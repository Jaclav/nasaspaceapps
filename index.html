What is a solar storm?

A solar storm, despite its name does not resemble any storm on Earth. This magnetic eruption can cause a lot of trouble due to the fact that most of our current technology operates on electromagnetic waves. However, similarly to how there is a rainbow after a a regular storm, solar storm causes an equally astonishing phenomenon - aurora.

How does it work?

It all starts with a large-scale magnetic eruption that causes a burst of plasma from the Sun - Coronal mass ejection. CME is often assiociated with solar flare which looks like a volcano exploding in a pattern similar to what a magnet creates when put around iron filings. That is because the solar flare is an explosion caused by reorganization of the magnetic field lines. Furthermore, it accelerates charged particles to really high velocities and once they reach Earth's magnetosphere, they start messing with electromagnetic signals that we send.

Why care?

Carrington event was not the only black out event caused by sun. In 1989 in Quebec, Canada solar storm caused 9 hours of black out and costed 20 milion CAD. It was caused by lack of preparations.
